Canon EOS 1100D

The Canon EOS 1100D (also known as the Digital Rebel T3) is the new entry-level model in Canon's extensive range of digital SLR cameras, replacing the 3 year old 1000D model. Aimed at first-time DSLR users, the 1100D inherits features from both the equally new and more expensive EOS 600D and the previous 1000D. It has a 12 megapixel CMOS sensor, 2.7 inch LCD screen, 3fps continuous shooting mode, 9 autofocus points, 63-zone iFCL exposure metering, ISO 100-6400 sensitivity, on-screen Feature Guide, and 720p HD video capture.

Canon EOS 1100D 18-55mm Lens

The EF-S 18-55mm f/3.5-5.6 III is a standard zoom lens perfect for those wanting to start exploring DSLR photography. Good for most shooting situations. The aspheric lens element produces images that are distortion corrected, ensuring consistent image quality over the frame. Combined with the DC autofocus motor, a high-speed CPU and improved AF algorithm, you can be sure to get a sharp photo.

Canon EOS 1100D 75-300mm Lens

A compact and lightweight telephoto zoom, the Canon EF 75-300 f/4-5.6 III offers a high magnification also allowing anyone to be able to enjoy taking portraits with excellent background blur thanks to a circular aperture opening, as well as sports and wildlife photos.
